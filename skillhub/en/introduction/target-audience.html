Understanding Your Customer

OPCP isn't a one-size-fits-all product. Different people in an organisation care about different things. A CTO thinks about technology strategy, while a compliance officer worries about regulations. Knowing who you're talking to helps you highlight the benefits that matter most to them.

Think of it like selling a car: a parent cares about safety features, a business executive cares about fuel efficiency and image, and a teenager cares about speed. Same car, different conversations.

OPCP (OVHcloud Hosted Private Cloud) provides dedicated computing resources managed by OVHcloud — understanding who benefits most helps you tailor the conversation.

Key Audience Roles

IT Managers

What they care about: Day-to-day operations, uptime, team workload, and reducing complexity.

Scenario: An IT manager at a logistics company is overwhelmed maintaining ageing on-premise servers. Their small team spends 60% of their time on hardware maintenance instead of strategic projects. With OPCP, hardware management shifts to OVHcloud, freeing the team to focus on business-critical applications and innovation. Nodes are provisioned and managed without the team needing to handle physical hardware.

Chief Technology Officers (CTOs)

What they care about: Technology strategy, scalability, competitive advantage, and long-term architecture decisions.

Scenario: A CTO at a fast-growing SaaS company needs infrastructure that scales with their customer base without requiring a complete re-architecture every 18 months. OPCP provides dedicated resources that can be scaled up as the business grows, with a clear upgrade path that doesn't disrupt existing services.

Compliance Officers / Data Protection Officers

What they care about: Regulatory compliance, data location, audit trails, and risk management.

Scenario: A compliance officer at a financial institution must demonstrate to regulators that customer data is stored exclusively within the EU and is physically isolated from other organisations. OPCP's European data centres and dedicated hardware provide the documentation and guarantees needed to pass regulatory audits.

Chief Financial Officers (CFOs)

What they care about: Budget predictability, total cost of ownership, and return on investment.

Scenario: A CFO at a media company is frustrated by unpredictable cloud bills that spike during content launches. OPCP's fixed pricing model lets them budget IT infrastructure costs with confidence, turning a variable expense into a predictable line item.

Project Managers

What they care about: Timelines, resource availability, and reducing dependencies on external teams.

Scenario: A project manager launching a new digital product needs guaranteed infrastructure availability from day one. With OPCP, resources are provisioned and ready on a predictable timeline, removing infrastructure delays from the project's critical path.

Conversation Starters by Role

When speaking with different stakeholders, lead with what matters to them:

  • IT Manager: "How much time does your team spend on hardware maintenance versus strategic projects?"
  • CTO: "What's your infrastructure scaling plan for the next 3 years?"
  • Compliance Officer: "Where is your customer data physically stored today, and can you prove it to auditors?"
  • CFO: "How predictable are your monthly cloud infrastructure costs?"
  • Project Manager: "How long does it take to get new infrastructure provisioned for a project?"
