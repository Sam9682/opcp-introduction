Instance Setup

Intermediate

Overview

Setting up a new instance in OPCP is similar to moving into a new office space. You choose the size and layout you need, confirm the details, and then the space is prepared for you. This page walks you through the end-to-end workflow at a conceptual level — no technical commands required.

Real-world analogy: Think of instance setup like ordering a custom-built computer from a catalogue. You select the specifications you need, confirm your order, and the system is assembled and delivered ready to use.

The Setup Workflow

Creating a new instance follows a clear, step-by-step process through the management interface. Here is what each stage involves:

Step 1: Choose a Template

Begin by selecting a pre-configured template that matches your workload needs. Templates are like floor plans for an apartment — each one comes with a recommended combination of processing power, memory, and storage suited to a particular type of work.

  • General Purpose — Balanced resources for everyday business applications
  • Compute-Optimised — Extra processing power for data-intensive tasks
  • Memory-Optimised — Additional memory for applications that handle large datasets simultaneously
  • Storage-Optimised — Expanded storage capacity for archiving or media-heavy workloads

Step 2: Configure Resources

After selecting a template, you fine-tune the resource allocation. This is like adjusting the furniture layout after choosing your apartment — you decide exactly how much of each resource you need.

  • Processing capacity — How much CPU power is dedicated to your instance
  • Memory allocation — How much RAM is available for active tasks
  • Storage volume — How much storage space is assigned for your data
  • Network connectivity — Which networks your instance can communicate with

Step 3: Set Naming and Organisation

Give your instance a meaningful name and assign it to a project or department. This is like labelling rooms in a building — it helps everyone find and manage resources efficiently.

  • Choose a descriptive name that identifies the purpose (e.g., "Marketing Analytics" or "Customer Portal")
  • Assign to the appropriate organisational group for billing and access control
  • Add optional tags for easier searching and filtering later

Step 4: Review and Confirm

Before the instance is created, you see a summary of all your choices — template, resources, naming, and estimated costs. This is your chance to double-check everything, much like reviewing an order before clicking "Purchase".

  • Verify the selected template and resource levels
  • Confirm the naming and organisational assignment
  • Review the estimated monthly cost
  • Approve the setup to begin provisioning

Step 5: Deployment

Once confirmed, the platform automatically provisions your instance. This typically takes a few minutes — similar to waiting for a new phone to finish its initial setup. You will receive a notification when the instance is ready for use.

  • The system allocates the requested resources from available capacity
  • Network connections are established automatically
  • The instance appears in your dashboard with a "Ready" status
  • You can begin using it immediately or hand it off to your technical team

What to Tell Customers

  • Instance setup is a guided, visual process — no technical expertise required to initiate
  • Templates simplify decision-making by offering pre-validated configurations
  • The entire process from selection to a running instance typically takes under 10 minutes
  • Costs are transparent and visible before any commitment is made
  • Instances can be modified after creation if needs change
