What Can CloudStore Do?

CloudStore isn't just a place to dump files. It's a sophisticated storage platform with capabilities designed to keep your data safe, accessible, and ready to grow with your business. Think of it as a warehouse that can automatically expand, has multiple backup locations, and offers different doors for different types of deliveries.

Capability 1: Scalability

Scalability means the ability to grow (or shrink) your storage space on demand, without downtime or disruption.

Real-world analogy: Imagine a warehouse where walls can slide outward whenever you need more room. You never have to move to a bigger building — your current one simply expands. When you need less space, the walls slide back and you stop paying for the unused area.

What this means for customers

  • Start small and grow as your data needs increase — no upfront over-provisioning.
  • Handle seasonal spikes (e.g., holiday sales data) without permanent capacity commitments.
  • No migration headaches — your data stays in place while capacity adjusts around it.

Capability 2: Redundancy

Redundancy means your data is automatically copied to multiple locations, so if one copy is lost or damaged, others remain intact.

Real-world analogy: Think of it like keeping copies of your most important documents in three different safes — one at home, one at the bank, and one at your lawyer's office. If any one location has a problem, you still have two other copies.

What this means for customers

  • Data survives hardware failures — a broken disk doesn't mean lost files.
  • Protection against localised incidents (power outages, network issues).
  • No manual backup copying required — redundancy happens automatically in the background.

Capability 3: Access Methods

Access methods are the different ways your applications and users can connect to and interact with stored data.

Real-world analogy: A well-designed warehouse has multiple entrances — a loading dock for large deliveries, a front desk for small pickups, and a secure vault door for high-value items. Each entrance serves a different purpose and type of visitor.

Available access methods

  • Web dashboard — browse and manage files through a visual interface, like using a file explorer on your computer.
  • API access — applications connect directly to read and write data automatically, without human interaction.
  • Secure transfer protocols — move large amounts of data efficiently using optimised transfer channels.

Capability 4: Encryption

Encryption is the process of scrambling data so that only authorised parties with the correct key can read it.

Real-world analogy: Imagine writing a letter in a secret code that only you and the recipient understand. Even if someone intercepts the letter, they see only meaningless symbols. Encryption does the same for your digital data.

What this means for customers

  • Data is protected both while stored (at rest) and while being transferred (in transit).
  • Meets compliance requirements for sensitive industries (healthcare, finance, legal).
  • Even in the unlikely event of unauthorised access to hardware, data remains unreadable.

Capabilities at a Glance

Capacity Tiers

CloudStore offers different capacity tiers to match your business size and needs. Think of these as different warehouse sizes — you pick the one that fits today and upgrade when you outgrow it.

Tier Best For Capacity Range Typical Use
Starter Small teams, startups Up to 1 TB Document storage, small application databases, team file sharing
Professional Growing businesses, mid-size companies 1 TB – 50 TB Media archives, multiple application backends, regular backups
Enterprise Large organisations, data-intensive industries 50 TB and above Big data analytics, compliance archives, multi-region replication

Tip for customer conversations: Start by understanding the customer's current data volume and growth rate. A business generating 100 GB per month will outgrow the Starter tier within a year — recommend Professional from the start to avoid an early migration.

Key Takeaways

  • Scalability lets customers grow storage without disruption or migration.
  • Redundancy automatically protects data by maintaining multiple copies.
  • Access methods provide flexible ways to interact with data (dashboard, API, transfer protocols).
  • Encryption keeps data secure both at rest and in transit.
  • Three capacity tiers (Starter, Professional, Enterprise) match different business sizes and budgets.
