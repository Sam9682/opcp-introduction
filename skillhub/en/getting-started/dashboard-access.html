Dashboard Access

Beginner

Overview

The OVHcloud Manager is your central control panel for all OPCP services. Think of it as the reception desk of a building — it is the single entry point where you check in and get directed to everything you need.

This lesson shows you how to reach the dashboard, what you need to log in, and what you will see when you arrive.

Entry Point URL

The OPCP dashboard is accessed through the OVHcloud Manager at:

https://www.ovh.com/manager

Bookmark this address in your browser for quick access. This is the same URL used by all OVHcloud customers worldwide — once you log in, you will see only your own services and data.

Login Prerequisites

Before you can access the dashboard, make sure you have:

  • An active OVHcloud account — If you have not created one yet, see the Account Setup lesson
  • Your login email — The email address you used during registration
  • Your password — The password you created for your account
  • Two-factor authentication device (if enabled) — A phone or authenticator app for the security code

Step 1: Navigate to the Login Page

Open your browser and go to https://www.ovh.com/manager. You will see the OVHcloud login screen with fields for your email and password.

Step 2: Enter Your Credentials

Type your registered email address and password. If you have enabled two-factor authentication, you will be prompted for a verification code after entering your password.

Step 3: Arrive at the Dashboard

After successful login, you land on the OVHcloud Manager dashboard. This is your home base — a summary view showing your active services, recent activity, and quick links to common actions.

What You See on the Dashboard

The dashboard landing page is organised into clear sections:

  • Service summary cards — Quick counts of your active compute, storage, and network resources
  • Recent activity — A timeline of recent changes, orders, and support tickets
  • Quick actions — Shortcuts to common tasks like ordering new services or viewing invoices
  • Navigation menu — The left-side panel that takes you to specific service categories (covered in the next lesson)

Common Access Issues

If you cannot log in, here are the most common reasons and solutions:

  • Forgotten password — Click "Forgot password?" on the login page to receive a reset email
  • Account locked — After several failed attempts, your account may be temporarily locked. Wait 15 minutes and try again
  • Two-factor code not working — Ensure your device clock is synchronised. Authenticator codes are time-sensitive
