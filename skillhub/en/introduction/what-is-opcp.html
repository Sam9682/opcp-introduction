The Private Apartment Analogy

Imagine you need a place to live in a big city. You have three options:

  • A shared hostel — cheap, but you share everything with strangers. Noisy, unpredictable, and you have no control over who comes and goes.
  • Building your own house — total control, but incredibly expensive. You handle plumbing, electricity, security, and maintenance all by yourself.
  • A private apartment in a managed building — your own space with your own lock, but the building management handles security, maintenance, and utilities. You get privacy and control without the burden of running everything yourself.

OPCP is like that private apartment. It gives your business its own dedicated computing space inside OVHcloud's professionally managed infrastructure. You get the privacy and performance of owning your own equipment, without the cost and complexity of building and maintaining a data centre.

What Does "Hosted Private Cloud" Mean?

Let's break down the name into everyday language:

Hosted

Someone else provides and maintains the physical equipment. Just like a hotel provides the bed, towels, and room service — OVHcloud provides the servers, storage, and networking hardware. You don't need to buy, install, or repair anything physical.

Private

The resources are exclusively yours. Unlike shared hosting (the hostel), no other company's workloads run on your hardware. This means consistent performance, stronger security, and full control over your environment.

Cloud

You access and manage everything over the internet through a web dashboard. There's no need to visit a data centre or plug in cables. You can scale up or down, configure settings, and monitor performance from anywhere.

How OPCP Fits In

Key Takeaways

  • OPCP stands for OVHcloud's Hosted Private Cloud — dedicated computing resources managed by OVHcloud on your behalf.
  • It combines the security and performance of private infrastructure with the convenience of a managed service.
  • You don't need technical expertise to understand or discuss OPCP — think of it as your company's private apartment in OVHcloud's building.
  • Your data stays in European data centres, helping meet compliance and data sovereignty requirements.
